Curb Cut — ADA Survey Tool

Curb cuts — the small ramps where sidewalks meet streets — are legally required under the Americans with Disabilities Act. When they're missing, broken, or too steep, people who use wheelchairs, walkers, or strollers can be completely blocked from moving through their own neighborhood.

This tool helps you map problem locations, document them with photos, and generate a formal complaint letter addressed to your local public works department — so your findings can actually drive change.

Here's how it works

1
Search for your street or neighborhood to zoom the map there.
2
Drop pins on every missing or broken curb cut you find. Add a photo and description to each one.
3
Fill in your details — your name, the city, and who the letter should go to.
4
Generate a PDF — a formal ADA complaint letter with your map, photos, and every location documented.
